How OT Bidding Works

This chart helps us understand when works best for you. The more availability you share, the better we can match your overtime to slots that fit your schedule — so you get hours that work for your life, not just whatever's left.

  1. Go through each week using the arrows in the header (or ← → keys). Your scheduled shift is in the center of the grid — open slots are above and below it.
  2. Click a cell to mark it. It cycles: PREFER → CAN → CAN'T → empty. You can also click and drag across multiple cells to select them all at once.
  3. PREFER — you want to work this slot. CAN — you're available if needed. CAN'T — you're not available. Empty — no preference.
  4. Mark as many slots as you can. The more availability you give, the better chance we can cover every slot and give you the hours you want. The system only assigns OT where there's an actual staffing need — so you won't get assigned to a block that's already full.
  5. Round 1 (4 hrs/wk) — everyone gets up to 4 hours of OT per week. The system fills the hardest-to-cover slots first. If you're the only one available for a scarce slot, you'll get it. If many people are available, the system picks the person whose options are most limited — saving flexible people for other gaps.
  6. Round 2 (your choice) — want more OT? Enter how many extra hours per week you'd like in the Desired Hours section at the bottom. The system will assign additional shifts up to your desired amount, again prioritizing hard-to-fill slots and balancing hours evenly.
  7. Why mark more? The system is smart — it won't overload you. But if you only mark one or two slots, you might miss out if those slots have lots of competition. Marking more gives the system flexibility to find you hours.
  8. Grey cells are your regular shift — you can't get OT for blocks you're already working.
  9. Cross-position coverage: OT is assigned by priority — Dispatch first, then PIC, then Calltaking. If you get assigned dispatch or PIC OT, you won't be double-booked for calltaker OT on the same block. The system tracks assignments across all positions.
  10. When you're done with all weeks, hit SUBMIT BIDS. You can come back and resubmit anytime before the deadline.
