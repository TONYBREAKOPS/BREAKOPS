Reading the Diagram

  • Blue boxes = Data sources (where information comes from)
  • Purple boxes = Computation steps (where the system does math)

Key takeaway: The system pulls from multiple data sources automatically. Employee schedules, vacations, rotation assignments, and staffing targets are all factored in before any OT slot is identified. The grid shows you exactly where the gaps are.

Summary Bar

At the top of the grid, a summary bar shows the total number of OT slots needed for the entire month and the total OT hours those slots add up to. This gives you a quick snapshot of how much overtime the month requires.

Tip: Start by looking at the NEEDED badges. These are the gaps you need to fill. The summary bar tells you how big the problem is at a glance.

OT Bids Row

Below the main staffing grid, there is a purple row that shows employee bids for each time block. This is the OT Bids Row.

What You Will See

Color / BadgeWhat It Means

YellowThe employee marked this block as CAN — they are available but did not specifically request it.
Red with OT badgeThe employee has been assigned to work overtime in this block (after distribution and publish).

Only employees who are qualified for the current role are shown. For example, if you are viewing the Calltaker role, you will see all employees (since everyone can fill calltaker). If you are viewing PIC, you will only see PICs.

Remember: The bids row updates in real time. As employees submit their bids, their preferences appear here automatically. After you publish, assigned employees turn red.

Three Roles

The system manages overtime for three separate roles. Each role has its own pool of employees who can fill it. The roles are handled in a specific order because some employees can fill more than one role.

RoleWho Can Fill ItPool SizeRun OrderWhy This Order
PICOnly PICsSmallestFirstFewest people available, so fill these slots first before anyone gets used elsewhere.
DispatcherPICs + DispatchersMediumSecondPICs can step down to dispatch. Fill these after PIC slots are covered.
CalltakerEveryone (PICs + Dispatchers + Calltakers)LargestLastAnyone can take calls. Fill these last since the pool is biggest.

Switching Roles

At the top of the screen, you will see role switcher buttons (PIC, Dispatcher, Calltaker). Click any button to switch the grid to that role. The grid will reload with that role's staffing targets, employees, and OT needs.

Important: Each role has its own separate set of staffing targets and OT calculations. When you switch roles, you are looking at a completely different view of the same month.

Manual OT Assignment

Sometimes you need to assign overtime by hand instead of using the distribution engine. Maybe a slot did not get filled, or you know exactly who should work it. Here is how to do it.

To Assign Someone

1 Find the NEEDED badge on the grid (for example, "CT NEEDED" or "DP NEEDED").

2 Click on the NEEDED badge. A popup will appear.

3 Look at the dropdown list. It is grouped by position. Next to each name, you will see their bid preference for that slot (PREFER, CAN, or no bid).

4 Pick a name from the dropdown.

5 Click ASSIGN. The person's name will appear on the grid with a red OT badge.

To Remove an Assignment

1 Find the OT chip on the grid (the name with the red OT badge).

2 Click on it. The assignment will be removed and the slot will go back to showing NEEDED.

Tip: The dropdown in the assignment popup shows bid preferences so you can see who actually wants to work that slot. Prefer people who marked PREFER before picking someone who marked CAN.

OT Bidding Panel

The OT Bidding panel is where you control the entire bidding process. This is the command center for setting up, running, and publishing overtime assignments.

How to Open It

Click the OT BIDDING button at the top of the screen. The panel will slide open.

Settings

SettingWhat It Does

Period End DateThe last day of the OT bidding period (usually the last day of the month).
DeadlineThe date and time when employees must have their bids submitted. After this time, no more bids are accepted.
StatusOpen (accepting bids), Closed (deadline passed), or Assigned (results published).
Block Size4 hours. Each time slot is a 4-hour block. This is fixed.
Default CapThe default monthly hour limit for each employee. This prevents any one person from getting too much overtime.

Bid List

Below the settings, you will see a list of employees who have submitted bids. It shows a submitted count (how many employees have turned in their availability card). Click on any employee's name to expand their row and see their slot-by-slot bids.

Employee Caps

Each employee has a per-person monthly hour limit. The default cap applies to everyone, but you can change it for individual employees. If someone has a medical restriction or a special agreement, you can lower or raise their cap.

Simulate Bids

The Simulate Bids button generates fake test data. Use this when you want to try out the distribution engine without waiting for real employees to submit bids. This is for testing only.

Save Settings

When you click Save Settings, the system saves your period dates, deadline, and caps. It also computes the OT need slots for all roles. This is an important step — you must save settings before running distribution.

Do not forget: You must click Save Settings at least once after setting your period dates. If you skip this step, the distribution engine will not have any slots to fill.

Distribution Algorithm (How Assignments Are Made)

When you run the distribution, the system uses a set of rules to decide who gets which overtime slot. Here is how it works, step by step, in plain language.

Round 1: Fair Share

The goal of Round 1 is to give everyone a fair, equal share of overtime. The weekly target is about 4 hours per week (which equals 1 block).

1 Hardest slots go first. The system looks at all the OT slots and picks the one with the fewest people who bid on it. That slot is the hardest to fill, so it gets handled first.

2 Person with fewest options gets priority. Among the people who bid on that slot, the person who has the fewest other slots they can work goes first. This prevents someone with lots of options from taking a slot away from someone who has very few choices.

3 PREFER beats CAN. If two people are tied, the one who marked PREFER wins over the one who marked CAN.

4 Seniority breaks ties. If two people have the same preference, the one with higher seniority (lower seniority number) gets the slot.

5 Automatic skips. The system will never assign someone to a slot if any of these are true:

  • They are already scheduled to work during that block
  • They are on vacation that day
  • They are already assigned to another role's OT in the same block (no double-booking)
  • They have hit their monthly hour cap

Round 2: Extra Hours

Some employees want more overtime than the fair share. If an employee set their Round 2 (R2) hours to a number greater than 0 in their bid, they are saying "I am willing to work extra beyond my fair share."

1 Same logic as Round 1 — hardest slot first, fewest options, PREFER beats CAN, seniority.

2 Expanded weekly cap. The weekly limit is raised to include the extra hours the employee requested.

3 Only people who asked for extra. If an employee did not set R2 hours, they are not considered in Round 2.

4 Monthly cap still applies. Even in Round 2, no one can go over their monthly hour limit.

In simple terms: Round 1 makes sure everyone gets a fair piece of the pie. Round 2 lets people who want more grab the leftovers, as long as they have not hit their limit.

Run All Roles

Instead of running the distribution for one role at a time, you can click the RUN ALL ROLES button. Here is what happens:

1 PIC runs first. The system loads PIC employees and PIC staffing targets, then runs the distribution for PIC slots.

2 Dispatcher runs second. The system loads Dispatcher employees (including PICs who can step down) and Dispatcher staffing targets. It knows which PICs were already assigned to PIC OT, so they will not be double-booked.

3 Calltaker runs last. The system loads all employees and Calltaker staffing targets. Anyone already assigned to PIC or Dispatcher OT in a given block is skipped.

After all three roles finish, you will see a combined results panel showing how many slots were filled and how many are still open for each role.

Why this order matters: PICs are the hardest to find because they are the smallest group. By filling PIC slots first, you make sure those critical positions are covered before PICs get pulled into Dispatcher or Calltaker OT.

Publishing

After you run the distribution (or make manual assignments), the results are just a preview. Nothing is final until you publish.

1 Review the results. Look at the results panel. Check fill rates. See if any critical slots are still unfilled. Manually assign anyone you need to.

2 Click PUBLISH. This saves all assignments to the database for the current role.

3 Assignments become visible everywhere:

  • On the monthly grid as red OT badges with employee names
  • On the OT Bids Row as red chips with OT badges
  • On each employee's OT Bidding page under their Approved Slots section
Be careful: Once you publish, the assignments are live. Employees can see them immediately. Make sure you are happy with the results before clicking PUBLISH.

Clear OT

If you need to start over, click the CLEAR OT button.

  • This removes all OT assignments for the current role and current month.
  • Employee bids stay intact. Nothing happens to the bids themselves. Only the assignments are erased.
  • The grid updates right away. NEEDED badges will reappear where assignments were removed.
When to use this: Use CLEAR OT when you want to re-run the distribution from scratch, or when you made a mistake and want to undo all assignments at once.

Export / Import

Export OT Needs

Click EXPORT OT NEEDS to download an Excel file (XLSX) of all unfilled OT slots for the current role and month. This is useful when you need to share the overtime needs with other supervisors or departments, or when you want to track unfilled slots outside the system.

Import OT Assignments

Click IMPORT OT ASSIGNMENTS to upload an Excel (XLSX) or CSV file with bulk assignments. This lets you assign overtime in bulk using a spreadsheet instead of clicking one slot at a time. The file must follow the expected format (matching employee names and time blocks).

Tip: Export first, fill in the names in the spreadsheet, then import it back. This is the fastest way to assign a large number of OT slots at once.

Special Detail (Dispatcher Only)

When you are viewing the Dispatcher role, you will see a purple SD row on the grid. This shows predicted Special Detail assignments.

  • What is Special Detail? Some dispatchers are pulled from their normal duties to work special assignments (SD). When that happens, they are not available to dispatch.
  • How it affects the grid: SD predictions reduce the dispatcher count for that block. If 5 dispatchers are scheduled but 1 is predicted for SD, the system counts only 4 dispatchers available.
  • Dispatcher overflow: When there are more dispatchers than needed in a block, the extra dispatchers "overflow" into the calltaker count. This shows up as a blue DISP IN CT badge on the calltaker grid.
Remember: Special Detail only appears on the Dispatcher view. It does not affect PIC or Calltaker views directly. However, the dispatcher overflow into calltaking does reduce calltaker OT needs.

Vacation Handling

Vacations are pulled automatically from VacationOps. You do not need to enter them manually.

  • On the Bids Row: Vacation dates appear in cyan (light blue) at the bottom of each cell. This lets you see at a glance who is off.
  • During distribution: The system automatically skips employees who are on vacation for a given day. They will never be assigned OT on a vacation day.
  • In the staffing count: Employees on vacation are removed from the scheduled staff count for that day. This increases the shortfall and may create additional OT needs.
Make sure VacationOps is up to date. If a vacation is not entered in VacationOps, the system will not know about it. The employee could be assigned OT on a day they are actually off.

Step-by-Step Workflow

Follow these steps in order to manage overtime for a month. Repeat for each role if you are running them individually.

1 Switch to the role you want to manage. Click PIC, Dispatcher, or Calltaker at the top of the screen.

2 Review the monthly grid. Look at where NEEDED badges appear. These are the gaps that need overtime coverage.

3 Open the OT Bidding panel. Click the OT BIDDING button. Set the period start date, end date, and deadline.

4 Save settings. Click Save Settings. This computes the OT need slots for all roles. You must do this before running distribution.

5 Wait for employees to submit bids. Employees will fill out their availability cards on the OT Bidding page. You can check progress in the bid list. (Or use Simulate Bids for testing.)

6 Click RUN ALL ROLES (or RUN DISTRIBUTION for a single role). The system will match bids to OT needs using the distribution algorithm.

7 Review the results panel. Check fill rates and see which slots are still unfilled.

8 Manually assign any remaining unfilled slots if needed. Click on NEEDED badges to open the assignment popup.

9 Click PUBLISH to commit the assignments. They are now saved and visible to everyone.

10 Repeat for other roles if you ran them individually instead of using RUN ALL ROLES.

Troubleshooting

If something does not look right, check this list before calling for help.

"No bid data available"
This means the system does not have any OT need slots computed yet. Open the OT Bidding panel and click Save Settings first. Make sure your period dates are set.
"Period dates not set"
You need to enter a start date and end date in the OT Bidding panel settings. Without these dates, the system does not know which month to compute.
Distribution seems slow or the screen freezes briefly
This is normal for large datasets. The distribution engine is doing a lot of math (checking every employee against every slot). Give it a moment. The screen may freeze for a few seconds, but it will finish.
Over-staffing is showing when it should not be
Check whether you published assignments more than once without clearing first. If you run distribution and publish, then run again and publish again without clearing, the assignments can stack up. Use CLEAR OT first, then re-run and re-publish.
Bids are not showing in the grid
Make sure employees submitted their bids after you saved the settings. If the settings were saved after the employee submitted, their bids may not line up with the computed slots. Have the employee re-submit, or click Save Settings again.
A specific employee is not appearing in the assignment dropdown
Check that the employee's position matches the role you are viewing. A calltaker will not appear in the PIC assignment dropdown. Also check that they are not on vacation for that day.
DISP IN CT badges are not showing on the calltaker grid
Make sure RotationOps has the dispatcher calltaking assignments entered for the current month. The overflow calculation depends on RotationOps data being present.
After clearing OT, the old assignments still show
Try refreshing the page. The grid should update immediately, but in rare cases a page refresh is needed.
Still stuck? Take a screenshot of what you see and send it to the system administrator. Include the role you were viewing, the month, and what you were trying to do.
