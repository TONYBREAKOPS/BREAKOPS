Data Flow: Step by Step

Here is exactly what happens, in order, when the system determines OT needs and makes assignments:

1 Employee Database — The system reads every employee's name, position (PIC, Dispatcher, Calltaker), seniority number, and weekly schedule (e.g., 6A-2P Mon-Fri, OFF Sat-Sun).

2 Adequate Staffing Targets — Each role has its own staffing requirements. For example, "Calltakers need 5 people from 6A-10A on weekdays." These are stored per day-of-week and per hour block.

3 Vacation List — VacationOps tracks who is on vacation and when. Anyone on vacation is removed from the staffing count for those dates. The distribution algorithm also automatically excludes employees on vacation — you will never be assigned OT on a day you're listed as on vacation.

4 Staffing Calculation — For each day of the month, for each 4-hour block: the system counts how many employees are scheduled to work, subtracts vacations, and compares to the adequate staffing target. If scheduled < target, the difference is the OT need.

5 RotationOps (Calltaker only) — Some dispatchers are assigned to calltaking duty on specific days through RotationOps. These count as additional calltaker coverage and reduce calltaker OT needs.

6 Manual OT / Previously Published OT — Any OT already assigned (manually or from a previous distribution) is subtracted from the remaining need.

7 Employee Bids — Employees submit their availability (PREFER / CAN / CAN'T) for each time block, plus their desired Round 1 and Round 2 hours.

8 Distribution Engine — The algorithm matches employee bids to OT needs using the priority rules (scarcest slot first, fewest options, PREFER > CAN, seniority). Round 1 ensures fair share; Round 2 fills remaining slots.

9 Run All Roles — When supervisors click "Run All Roles," the engine runs three times in order: PIC first, then Dispatcher, then Calltaker. Each role loads its own employee list and staffing targets. Assignments from earlier roles carry forward to prevent double-booking.

10 Publish — Supervisor reviews the results and clicks Publish. Assignments are saved to the database and become visible on the staffing grid (red OT badges), the OT Bids row (bubbles turn red), and on each employee's OT Bidding page (Approved Slots).

Why We're Doing This

We're replacing the old overtime assignment process with a preference-based bidding system. Instead of supervisors guessing who's available, you tell us when you want to work.

The bottom line: The more availability you submit, the better chance you have of getting the OT shifts you actually want — on your terms, on your schedule.

What's In It For You

  • You choose your shifts — Mark which blocks you prefer, which you can work, and which you can't
  • Fair distribution — The system uses seniority and scarcity to assign shifts fairly
  • No surprises — You'll see exactly what you were assigned before the schedule goes live
  • More availability = more OT — The system can only assign you to shifts you bid on
  • Less mandatory OT — When more people volunteer, fewer people get mandated
If you don't bid, you won't get voluntary OT. Only employees who submit their availability card will be considered for OT assignments. Unfilled slots may result in mandatory overtime for someone else.

Using the Bidding App

What You'll See on Screen

After logging in, the main screen has these areas:

  1. Header bar — Your name, position, week navigation arrows, deadline countdown, "How It Works" button, and Sign Out
  2. Schedule bar — Shows your regular work schedule for the current week (e.g., "6A-2P" on your work days, "OFF" on your days off). This tells you which blocks you're already working so you know where to bid for OT.
  3. Color legend — Shows the four cell states: PREFER (green), CAN (yellow), CAN'T (red), and SCHED (grey = your regular shift)
  4. Sidebar: Round tracker cards — Shows live counts of your PREFER, CAN, CAN'T, and TOTAL bids
  5. Bidding grid — The main table with days across the top and 6 time blocks down the left side
  6. Footer bar — Desired Hours inputs, unsaved changes status, and the SUBMIT BIDS button

The Bidding Grid

The grid shows one week at a time. Each column is a day, and each row is a 4-hour time block. Each cell represents one OT slot you can bid on.

  • Grey cells — These are blocks during your regular shift. You can't bid on them because you're already working.
  • Empty cells — Available for bidding. Click or drag to set your preference.
  • Green cells (PREFER) — You've marked this as a shift you want.
  • Yellow cells (CAN) — You've marked this as available if needed.
  • Red cells (CAN'T) — You've marked this as unavailable.

PIC Priority Badges (PIC Users Only)

If you are a PIC, you will see colored role badges on each cell in the bidding grid. These badges tell you which roles need OT coverage for that specific slot.

  • Badges show role labels — Each cell displays a badge like "PIC", "DP", "CT", or a combined label like "PIC/DP/CT" if multiple roles need coverage in that slot.
  • PIC slots are highlighted — Slots that need PIC coverage have a priority indicator so you can spot them quickly.
  • Use badges to guide your bids — Aim to mark PREFER on slots that show a PIC label. This gives you the best chance of being assigned as PIC for that block.
  • Bid on other slots too — Even if a slot only shows "DP" or "CT", it's still worth bidding on as backup availability.
Getting PIC OT is not guaranteed. Even if you bid on a PIC-labeled slot, you may be assigned as Dispatcher or Calltaker for that block depending on overall demand and other bids. The badges help you make informed decisions, but the final role assignment depends on the distribution engine.

How to Set Your Preferences

Method 1: Click to Cycle

Click any empty cell and it will cycle through the options in this order:

Empty → PREFER → CAN → CAN'T → Empty → ...

Keep clicking to reach the option you want.

Method 2: Click and Drag (Recommended for Multiple Cells)

This is the fastest way to fill out your card:

  1. Click and hold on any cell
  2. Drag across multiple cells — they'll be highlighted with a blue border
  3. Release the mouse — a popup menu appears with four buttons: PREFER, CAN, CAN'T, and CLEAR
  4. Click your choice — all selected cells are set at once
Pro tip: Use drag-to-select for filling out entire days at once. For example, drag from 6A-10A down to 2A-6A on your day off, then click PREFER to mark the whole day.

Navigating Between Weeks

The grid shows one week at a time. Use the arrow buttons in the header to move between weeks:

  • Click ◀ (left arrow) to go to the previous week
  • Click ▶ (right arrow) to go to the next week
  • You can also use keyboard arrow keys (← and →) to navigate weeks
  • The header shows which week you're on (e.g., "Week 2 of 5")
Don't forget the other weeks! Make sure you go through every week of the month, not just the first one. Use the arrows to check all weeks before submitting.

Setting Your Desired Hours

At the bottom of the screen, in the footer bar, you'll see the Desired Hours section:

InputWhat It ControlsDetails
Round 1 Your fair-share weekly allotment Fixed at 4 hours/week (1 block). This cannot be changed — it ensures everyone gets a fair share before anyone gets extra.
Round 2 Extra OT you'd like beyond the fair share Round 2 is opt-in only. It only runs for employees who set Round 2 desired hours greater than 0. Enter how many additional hours you want. If you want as much OT as possible, set this high. If you only want the 4-hour fair share from Round 1, leave it at 0 and you won't be considered for extra hours.
Round 2 hours are not guaranteed. The system will try to give you up to your desired amount, but it depends on how many slots are available and how many other people bid. The monthly cap (default 36 hours) always applies.

  • Review all weeks using the navigation arrows
  • Make sure you've set your Round 2 desired hours

Filling Out Your Availability Card

The Four Options

For each time block on each day, you have four choices:

OptionColorWhat It MeansWhen to Use It
PREFER Green You want this shift. This is your first choice. Days off when you're free and want to work. Blocks that fit your schedule best.
CAN Yellow You can work this shift if needed, but it's not your first pick. Times you're available but wouldn't choose first. Backup availability.
CAN'T Red You cannot work this shift. You have plans, appointments, family obligations, or are otherwise unavailable.
(blank) None You haven't responded for this block. The system skips blank blocks — they're treated as no bid. Fill in every block so supervisors know your full availability.
PREFER vs CAN matters. The system prioritizes PREFER bids over CAN bids. If two people bid for the same slot and one marked PREFER while the other marked CAN, the PREFER bid gets priority (all else being equal).

Time Blocks

Each day is divided into six 4-hour blocks:

BlockTimeTypical Shift Coverage
6A - 10A6:00 AM - 10:00 AMEarly morning
10A - 2P10:00 AM - 2:00 PMMid-day
2P - 6P2:00 PM - 6:00 PMAfternoon
6P - 10P6:00 PM - 10:00 PMEvening
10P - 2A10:00 PM - 2:00 AMLate night
2A - 6A2:00 AM - 6:00 AMOvernight
Each block = 4 hours of OT. One block in Round 1 equals your weekly fair share. If you want more, set your Round 2 desired hours.

Tips for Filling Out Your Card

  1. Be generous with your availability. The more blocks you mark as PREFER or CAN, the more likely you are to get OT. The system can only assign you to blocks you bid on.
  2. Use PREFER for your best shifts. If you really want a specific day/block, mark it PREFER. Don't mark everything PREFER — be honest about what you truly want vs. what you can do.
  3. Mark your days off. Your days off are the best time to pick up OT. Mark those blocks as PREFER or CAN.
  4. Mark CAN'T if you can't work a block. This tells supervisors you're unavailable. Don't leave blocks blank — fill in every block so we have a complete picture of your availability.
  5. Use drag-to-select for speed. Click and drag across a whole day, then pick PREFER or CAN from the popup. It's much faster than clicking each cell individually.
  6. Check every week. Navigate through all weeks of the month before submitting. Bids not entered are bids not counted.
  7. Submit on time. Late cards are not accepted. Watch the countdown timer in the header.
Example: If you have Tuesdays and Wednesdays off and you'd love to pick up 8 hours of OT, mark 2-3 blocks on those days as PREFER and a couple more as CAN. That gives the system flexibility to slot you in where you're needed most.

How Assignments Are Made

The assignment system is designed to be fair, transparent, and efficient. Here's exactly how it works:

Priority Order by Role

Assignments are made in this order because some roles are harder to fill:

OrderRoleWhy First
1stPICSmallest pool — only PICs can fill PIC slots
2ndDispatcherMedium pool — PICs, Dispatchers, and Supervisors can fill
3rdCalltakerLargest pool — everyone qualifies
What this means for you: If you're a PIC, your PIC OT needs are filled first before you'd be considered for Dispatcher or Calltaker OT. This ensures the hardest-to-fill positions get covered.

How the System Picks Who Gets a Shift

When multiple people bid for the same slot, the system uses these tiebreakers in order:

  1. Scarcest slot fills first — If a shift only has 1 person who bid for it, that person gets it before a shift with 10 bidders. This ensures hard-to-fill shifts don't go empty.
  2. Fewest options = higher priority — If you only bid for 3 blocks total and someone else bid for 20, you get priority on overlapping slots. The system protects people with limited availability.
  3. PREFER beats CAN — If you marked PREFER and someone else marked CAN, you win.
  4. Seniority — When all else is equal, seniority determines who gets the shift.

Round 1 vs Round 2

RoundGoalYour Weekly Cap
Round 1Give everyone a fair share of OT4 hours per week (1 block)
Round 2Fill remaining open slotsUp to your personal OT cap (default 36 hrs/month)

Round 1 makes sure everyone who wants OT gets at least some before anyone gets a lot. Round 2 is opt-in only — it only runs for employees who explicitly set their Round 2 desired hours above 0. If you only want the fair share (4 hours/week), leave Round 2 at 0. If you want extra OT, set Round 2 to how many additional hours you'd like.

Cross-Role Protection

The system runs all three roles in order (PIC → Dispatcher → Calltaker). Once you're assigned a slot in one role, you cannot be double-booked for that same time block in another role. For example:

  • If you're a PIC and get assigned PIC OT from 6A-10A on March 5, you will NOT also be assigned Dispatcher or Calltaker OT for that same time block.
  • The system tracks assignments across all roles to prevent conflicts.

What Won't Happen

  • You will never be assigned OT during your regular shift — the system knows your schedule
  • You will never be double-booked across roles (e.g., PIC OT and Calltaker OT at the same time)
  • You will never exceed your monthly OT cap
  • You will never be assigned to a block you didn't bid on
  • You will never be assigned to a block that's already fully staffed
  • You will never be assigned OT on a day you're on vacation — the system checks VacationOps automatically

Why Might a Slot Go Unfilled?

A slot may remain unfilled after distribution if:

  • Nobody bid for that time block
  • Everyone who bid for it was already assigned to another slot at the same time
  • All eligible bidders already hit their cap

Unfilled slots show as "NEEDED" on the staffing grid. Supervisors may assign them as mandatory OT.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I log in?
Select your name from the dropdown and enter your password. Your default password is the first 4 characters of your first name in lowercase (e.g., "jose" for Jose).
Q: What if I don't submit my card?
You will not be assigned any voluntary OT for that period. If staffing is still short after the bidding process, mandatory OT may be assigned regardless.
Q: Can I change my card after submitting?
Yes, you can update your availability any time before the deadline. After the deadline, changes are not accepted.
Q: How do I fill multiple cells at once?
Click and drag across the cells you want to select. They'll get a blue highlight. When you release, a popup appears with PREFER, CAN, CAN'T, and CLEAR buttons. Click one to apply it to all selected cells.
Q: What if I bid PREFER on everything?
The system treats them all equally as PREFER. It's better to be honest — mark your true preferences as PREFER and your backup availability as CAN. This helps the system make smarter assignments.
Q: What's the difference between CAN'T and blank?
CAN'T explicitly tells supervisors "I am not available for this block." Blank means you didn't respond. Both result in no assignment, but filling in CAN'T helps supervisors understand your actual availability. Fill in every cell.
Q: What if nobody bids for a shift?
That shift remains unfilled in the voluntary process. Supervisors may need to assign it as mandatory OT. The more people who bid, the less mandatory OT for everyone.
Q: Does seniority guarantee I get the shift I want?
Seniority is the last tiebreaker, not the first. Someone with fewer options and a PREFER bid can beat a senior person with a CAN bid and lots of availability. The system is designed to be fair, not just seniority-based.
Q: How much OT can I get?
The default cap is 36 hours per month. Your supervisor may set a different cap for you individually. Round 1 gives everyone up to 4 hours/week before Round 2 opens up more hours.
Q: What does the Round 2 "Desired Hours" input do?
Round 1 is fixed at 4 hours/week for everyone. Round 2 is opt-in only — it only runs for employees who set their Round 2 desired hours above 0. Enter how many extra hours you'd like and the system will try to give you up to that amount from remaining slots. If you leave it at 0, you will only receive up to 4 hours/week from Round 1.
Q: I'm a PIC. Will I get stuck filling Calltaker OT?
No. PIC OT needs are filled first. You'll only be considered for Dispatcher or Calltaker OT after all PIC slots are handled — and only if you bid for those time blocks.
Q: What if I get assigned a shift I marked CAN but don't really want?
CAN means you can work it. If you can't, mark it CAN'T. Be honest with your availability — the system takes you at your word.
Q: Can I see who else bid for the same shifts?
No. Bid submissions are confidential. You'll only see your own assignments after they're published.
Q: What happens to unfilled shifts?
They show up as "NEEDED" on the staffing grid. Supervisors can manually assign them or use mandatory OT. Help us avoid this — bid for what you can work.
Q: Why are some cells grey and unclickable?
Grey cells are time blocks during your regular scheduled shift. You can't bid for OT on blocks you're already working.
Q: What does the countdown timer in the header mean?
It shows how much time remains before the bidding deadline. When it turns red, you have less than 24 hours left. Once it hits zero, the SUBMIT button is disabled.
Q: I forgot to check all the weeks. Did I miss some?
Use the arrow buttons (or keyboard arrow keys) to navigate through all weeks. Any week you didn't fill out will have blank cells — the system won't bid for those blocks on your behalf.
Q: What does it mean when a bid bubble turns red in the OT BIDS row?
It means that person was assigned OT for that slot. Red bubbles with an OT badge match the red OT badges in the staffing row above. Green and yellow bubbles are bidders who were NOT assigned to that particular slot.
Q: What does "Run All Roles" do?
It runs the distribution engine three times in order: PIC first, then Dispatcher, then Calltaker. Each role uses its own employee list and staffing targets. This can be run from any role view — you don't need to switch between roles. Assignments from earlier roles carry forward so nobody gets double-booked.
Q: I'm a PIC. How do I know which slots need PIC coverage?
The bidding page shows role badges (PIC/DP/CT) on each cell telling you what's needed. Aim for PIC-labeled slots first, but bid on others too as backup.
Q: What if I'm on vacation during the bidding period?
Your vacation dates are automatically excluded. The system will not assign you OT on any day you're listed as on vacation in VacationOps.
Q: What if I set Round 2 to 0?
You'll only get up to 4 hours/week from Round 1. You won't be considered for extra hours in Round 2. Set Round 2 greater than 0 if you want more OT beyond the fair share.
